## Subheadings

Subheadings break up the flow of an article and give the reader landmarks to orient themselves. This section tests an h2, which is the most common subheading in a blog post.

### A Third-Level Heading

Third-level headings are useful for breaking a section into smaller chunks. They should be visually distinct from h2s but still clearly subordinate.

#### A Fourth-Level Heading

Fourth-level headings are rare in blog posts but worth styling. If you find yourself reaching for an h5 or h6, your outline may need restructuring.
